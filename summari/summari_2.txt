India's tiger population, which hit a record low of 1,411 in 2006, has more than doubled to 3,167 as of last year, accounting for about 70% of the world's wild tigers. Conservation efforts by the government's Project Tiger, operational since 1973, has resurrected the species, offering hope to other countries struggling to boost wildlife numbers. Prime Minister Narendra Modi hailed the good news while visiting two tiger reserves on Sunday to mark the program's 50th anniversary.
